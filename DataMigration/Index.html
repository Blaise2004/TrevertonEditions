Data Migration

>

My work looks at the hidden infrastructure behind our online lives, the systems that quietly track us, profile us, and turn our personal data into something profitable. I am interested in the severity of that surveillance, the way it operates just out of sight while shaping how we are seen, targeted, and understood by companies.

By pulling patents, diagrams, and extraction methods into view, I am revealing how these technologies map our behaviour, relationships, and decisions for commercial gain. It is easy to move through the internet without thinking about what we are giving away or who benefits from it. But the reality is that our digital selves are constantly being collected, repackaged, and sold.

This piece is my way of making that process visible, of confronting the quiet mechanisms built to capture us, and questioning the cost of living in a world where almost everything about us becomes data for someone else to use.
